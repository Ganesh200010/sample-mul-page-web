Robotic Operating Systems & Robot Simulation
Ros2

A ROS distribution is a versioned set of ROS packages. These are akin to Linux distributions (e.g. Ubuntu). Docs. The purpose of the ROS distributions is to let developers work against a relatively stable codebase until they are ready to roll everything forward.

Therefore once a distribution is released, we try to limit changes to bug fixes and non-breaking improvements for the core packages (every thing under ros-desktop-full). And generally that applies to the whole community, but for "higher" level packages, the rules are less strict, and so it falls to the maintainers of a given package to avoid breaking changes.
